# 🏢 C4 Infinity LLC: Acquisition SOP & Playbook (V2)

## 🎯 The "Buy Box" (Core Criteria)
*   **Financials:** Minimum $1M Annual Revenue | Minimum $300K SDE/EBITDA.
*   **Deal Size:** Up to $10M.
*   **Locations:** Greater Atlanta (1hr radius), Hollywood/Miami FL (1hr radius), or 100% Remote/Location-Agnostic.
*   **Target Industries:** Real Estate adjacent, Landscaping, B2B Bakeries. (Bonus: Strong recurring revenue).
*   **Excluded Industries:** Restaurants, Daycares, Pet Grooming/Vet (E-commerce/B2B to these sectors is OK).
*   **Financing:** Seller Financing (Primary) / Private Money PMLs/PMPs (Secondary). Avoid SBA.
*   **Operations:** Owner with an Operator from Day 1. (Buyer transition involvement: 1-2 weeks Full-Time).

---

## ⏱️ Daily Schedule (The 2-Hour Time Block)
*   **Block 1 (30 Mins):** Sourcing & AI Triage (DealScout AI)
*   **Block 2 (45 Mins):** Broker/Seller Outreach & Follow-ups
*   **Block 3 (45 Mins):** Deal Structuring, Legal Coordination & Capital Partner Comms

---

## 📋 Phase 1: Sourcing & AI Triage (30 Mins/Day)
1.  **Automated Filters:** Review daily email alerts from BizBuySell, BizQuest, and BizScout for target geographies and financials.
2.  **DealScout AI Evaluation:**
    *   Input the listing URL into DealScout AI.
    *   **Score < 50:** Immediate Pass. Delete and move on.
    *   **Score 50+:** Move to Phase 2. Add to your CRM as a "Lead" and initiate outreach to request financials.

## 📋 Phase 2: Outreach & Positioning (45 Mins/Day)
1.  **The Initial Message:** Send a standardized inquiry highlighting your 20+ years of retail/ops experience, your private capital backing, and your goal of preserving their legacy.
2.  **NDA & Document Request:** Sign the NDA immediately. Request the CIM, Trailing 3 Years P&L, and Tax Returns.
3.  **AI Financial Deep Dive:** Once financials are received, upload the PDFs into DealScout AI.
    *   *Prompt:* "Analyze this P&L. Identify the top 3 operational risks, customer concentration issues, and verify if the $300K SDE is accurate or inflated by add-backs."

## 📋 Phase 3: Negotiation & Legal Coordination (45 Mins/Day)
1.  **The Seller Call (Find the Pain):** Focus on building rapport and finding their "Why" (Retirement, burnout, health). Introduce the concept of Seller Financing as a way to avoid SBA red tape, save on taxes, and close faster.
2.  **Prepaid Legal Handoff (LOI Creation):**
    *   Once terms are loosely agreed upon verbally, send a bulleted list of the deal structure (Purchase Price, Down Payment, Seller Note Terms, Transition Period) to your **Prepaid Legal team**.
    *   Have them draft the formal non-binding Letter of Intent (LOI) to save you hours of drafting time.
3.  **Submit LOI:** Present the LOI to the seller/broker. Include a 60-day due diligence window.

## 📋 Phase 4: Due Diligence & Operator Identification
1.  **Financial QoE (Quality of Earnings):** Outsource the deep financial verification to a CPA/Fractional CFO to confirm the $300K SDE is real.
2.  **Operator Sourcing (The #2 Strategy):**
    *   *Primary Strategy:* During DD, ask the seller: *"Who runs the shop when you go on vacation?"* Identify the #2 employee (Manager, Lead Tech, Head Baker). Evaluate them for a promotion to GM/Operator.
    *   *Secondary Strategy:* If the #2 is not capable or doesn't exist, immediately begin interviewing external operators in the Atlanta/Miami area.
3.  **Capital Partner Pitch:** If PML/PMP capital is needed for the down payment, package the DealScout AI report and the Operator plan into a deck.

## 📋 Phase 5: Transition & Operations (Post-Close)
1.  **The 1-2 Week Sprint:** You are Full-Time on-site (Atlanta or Miami) for the first 7-14 days.
2.  **Elevating the #2:** Officially promote the #2 employee to Operator. Implement an incentive structure (profit share or phantom equity) to lock in their loyalty.
3.  **Knowledge Transfer:** Shadow the seller. Document every undocumented process.
4.  **Step Back:** Transition to a Chairman role. Implement a weekly 1-hour L10 meeting with your new Operator to review KPIs (Labor %, COGS, Revenue).

## 💡 Best Practices for Your Specific Model

### 1. Mastering the "#2 Promotion" Strategy
Promoting the #2 is the most efficient way to transition a business, but it carries risks. 
*   **The "Secret" Interview:** During Due Diligence, you usually can't talk to employees. Ask the seller to frame your visit as a "consultant" or "insurance inspector" so you can observe the #2 in action without spooking the staff.
*   **The Golden Handcuffs:** The #2 is used to a salary. To make them think like an owner, offer a base salary bump *plus* 10-15% of the net profit growth above the current baseline. This aligns their goals with yours and costs you nothing out of pocket on day one.

### 2. Maximizing Prepaid Legal
Prepaid legal is a massive asset for a 2-hour/day schedule, but they operate best with clear instructions.
*   **Create a "Term Sheet Template":** Keep a simple 1-page Word document with your standard SubTo/Seller Finance terms. When you get a verbal "yes" from a seller, fill in the blanks (Price, Interest Rate, Term Length) and send it to your legal team. Tell them: *"Turn this into an LOI."*
*   **Asset Purchase Agreement (APA):** Once the LOI is signed, have your legal team immediately start drafting the APA. Ensure they include standard M&A protections: Non-compete clauses, indemnification, and a right of offset (if the seller lied about debts, you deduct it from their seller finance note).

### 3. The "Skill Acquisition" Mindset for Deals 1 & 2
Since your first two deals are about building the muscle memory of M&A:
*   **Prioritize Downside Protection over Maximum Upside.** A deal with a 65 AI score that offers 90% seller financing with no personal guarantee is better for your first deal than an 85 AI score deal that requires you to drain your liquidity and sign a personal guarantee.
*   **Use the AI as your "Bad Cop":** When negotiating, use the DealScout AI reports to justify lower offers. *"Mr. Seller, my analysis software flagged a high customer concentration risk and declining margins over the last 12 months. Because of that risk, I can only offer X down."*

### 4. Direct-to-Seller Outreach (The "Blue Ocean")
To avoid the competitive bloodbath of BizBuySell:
*   Use 30 minutes of your weekend to pull a list of B2B Bakeries in Miami or Landscaping companies in Atlanta from Google Maps.
*   Use a tool like Hunter.io or TruePeopleSearch to find the owner's name and email/address.
*   Send a simple, direct message: *"Hi [Name], I'm a private buyer based in [Your City]. I have 20 years of retail ops experience and I'm looking to acquire a successful bakery in Miami to hold long-term. If you've ever considered transitioning out, I'd love to have a confidential chat. I am backed by private capital and can move quickly."*
